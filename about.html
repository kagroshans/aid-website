About

The story behind Alternative Investing Doctor — and why a practicing neurologist is writing about mortgage notes.

Who I Am

I'm Dr. Keith G., a board-certified neurologist in active clinical practice. I've spent years doing what every financial education source told me to do: max the 401(k), contribute to the backdoor Roth, dollar-cost average into index funds, eliminate consumer debt. The foundation stuff.

And it worked. The foundation is solid. But somewhere along the way, I started asking a question that White Coat Investor doesn't fully answer: what comes after the foundation?

Why Mortgage Notes

I came to mortgage note investing the way most physicians come to alternative investments — out of frustration. Rental properties sounded great until I understood what property management actually means. Real estate syndications lock up capital for five to ten years with projections that assume a permanently rising market. Private equity has a 30-year horizon and requires accredited investor status just to access mediocre returns.

Mortgage notes are different. When you buy a performing note, you become the bank. A borrower makes monthly payments to you. You don't own the property. You don't deal with tenants. You hold the debt instrument. The cash flow is predictable. The process is learnable.

What This Site Is

This is a documentation of my process, in real time. I'm not a retired physician looking back on a successful career as an investor. I'm a practicing neurologist actively screening notes, closing deals, and building a note portfolio alongside my clinical work.

Everything here is educational. Nothing here is investment advice. I share what I'm doing, why I'm doing it, and what I'm learning — so that other physicians can make informed decisions about whether this asset class fits their situation.

What I'm Not

  • Not a real estate broker or mortgage professional
  • Not selling a course or a fund
  • Not retired and looking back from a distance
